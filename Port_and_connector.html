Ports & Connectors

Explore the essential ports and connectors that power modern computing. Click on any card to expand and learn more!

USB Port & Connector

A USB Port is used to connect external devices like keyboards, mice, printers, and flash drives to a computer.

USB (Universal Serial Bus) supports data transfer and power supply through a single cable.

USB connectors include Type-A, Type-B, Type-C, Mini USB, and Micro USB.

It's one of the most widely used interfaces for peripheral connectivity.

VGA Port & Connector

A VGA Port is used to connect a computer to a monitor or projector for video output.

VGA (Video Graphics Array) transmits analog video signals.

The VGA connector has 15 pins arranged in three rows.

Though largely replaced by HDMI and DisplayPort, VGA is still used in older systems.

HDMI Port & Connector

An HDMI Port is used to transmit high-definition video and audio from a computer to a display or TV.

HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface) supports digital signals.

HDMI connectors come in Standard, Mini, and Micro sizes.

It is the most common interface for modern monitors, TVs, and gaming consoles.

Parallel Port & Connector

A Parallel Port is used to connect a computer to a printer or other peripheral devices.

The Parallel Port transmits data in parallel, allowing faster data transfer than serial ports.

Parallel ports are commonly used for connecting printers and other devices that require high-speed data transfer.

The parallel port typically has 25 pins and is often labeled as "LPT" (Line Printer Terminal).

Audio Jack Port & Connector

An Audio Jack Port is used to connect headphones, microphones, or speakers to a computer.

The most common size is the 3.5mm jack (also known as a headphone jack).

Color coding: Green for line-out/speakers, Pink for microphone, Blue for line-in.

Audio jacks are standard on computers, smartphones, and audio devices.

PS/2 Port & Connector

A PS/2 Port is used to connect keyboards and mice to a computer.

PS/2 connectors are circular with 6 pins and are color-coded: Purple for keyboard, Green for mouse.

Though largely replaced by USB, PS/2 is still used in some legacy systems.

PS/2 devices are not hot-swappable and require a restart to be recognized.

Serial Port & Connector

A Serial Port is used to transmit data one bit at a time between a computer and external devices.

Common serial ports include RS-232, DB9, and DB25 connectors.

Serial ports are used for modems, mice, and industrial equipment.

Though outdated, serial ports are still used in some legacy and industrial systems.

Power Cable Port & Connector

A Power Cable Port is used to supply electrical power to a computer or electronic device.

It connects the device to a main power source through a power cable.

The power connector (such as IEC C13/C14) ensures safe and stable power transfer.

Power cable ports are commonly found in PCs, monitors, printers, and SMPS units.

Ethernet Port & Connector

An Ethernet Port is used to connect a computer or device to a wired network for internet or data communication.

It supports LAN connections and provides stable, high-speed data transfer.

The Ethernet connector is an RJ-45 connector with 8 pins.

Ethernet ports are commonly used in PCs, routers, switches, and modems.

FireWire Port & Connector

A FireWire Port is a high-speed interface used to transfer data between a computer and external devices. It is commonly used for digital cameras, external hard drives, and audio equipment.

The FireWire connector comes in 4-pin, 6-pin, and 9-pin types.

FireWire supports fast and stable data transfer and is also known as IEEE 1394.

A FireWire Port (IEEE 1394) is a high-speed interface used to connect devices like digital cameras and external storage to a computer.
