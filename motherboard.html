Types Of Motherboard

Learn about essential computer motherboards. Discover types, features, and functions that power your PC.

Full AT Motherboard

The full AT motherboard from factor introduced by IBM in 1984.

The Full AT motherboard is 12" wide x 13.8" deep and only fits into full size AT tower case.

These was first type of motherboard , which were 12 inches wide and 11 inches long.

Baby AT Motherboard

The Baby AT motherboard from factor introduced by IBM in 1990`s.

The Baby AT motherboard is 8.57" wide x 13.04" deep which is almost the same as the origal IBM AT.

This motherboard was 10 inches wide and 8.5 inches long. and specially made for calssic Pentium and Pentium Pro processors.

ATX Motherboard

The ATX(Advanced Technology eXtended.) motherboard is a standard motherboard form factor introduced by Intel in 1995.

It provides a standardized layout for components, better airflow, and supports modern CPUs, RAM, expansion slots, and power management features.

It is commonly used in desktop computers because it offers good expandability and easy hardware upgrades.

LPX Motherboard

The LPX motherboard (Low Profile eXtension) is an older motherboard form factor designed for slim, low-profile desktop computers.

It uses a riser card to hold expansion slots, allowing the system to be compact.

LPX motherboards were popular in the 1990s but are now obsolete.

BTX Motherboard

The BTX motherboard (Balanced Technology eXtended) is a motherboard form factor developed by Intel. It was designed to improve cooling, airflow, and thermal management compared to ATX.

BTX uses a different layout to reduce heat from high-power components.

However, it was not widely adopted and was eventually discontinued.
